What is Canada Student Visa?

A Canadian student visa is one of the major prerequisites for a person to start his/her studies in Canada. Canada student visa is a combination of Canada Visitor Visa and study permit Or An electronic Travel Authorization (eTA) and study permit.

What can you do with canada student visa?

    A Canada student visa holder can

  • Start his/her studies in Canada.
  • Stay in Canada until the study programme enrolled ends.
  • Work (in some cases) while studying.
  • Work after graduation.

Benefits of Canada Student Visa

    Certain benefits are associated with the Canada Student Visa.

  • You become eligible for certain health benefits
  • Helps to easily get the Canadian issued documents.
  • You can easily get Canadian citizenship, however, you have to fulfill other eligibility criteria as well.

What are the eligibility criteria for a Canadian Student Visa?

    To apply for a Canada student visa, one must fulfil the following requirements

  • A Visa Application Center Consent form.
  • A valid passport.
  • A passport having a travel history and at least one blank page which is not the last page.
  • Court documents from your country stating your clean criminal history.
  • A declaration letter signed by you has to be submitted to the Canadian government wherein you have to explain why they should give you a student visa and study permit.
  • Proof of your employment or education status. It can be in the form of your resume or diploma/degree.
  • Bank statements proving that you have enough funds to carry out your studies and expenses in Canada.
  • A letter of explanation signed by you which states the reason for studying in Canada. Moreover, it should state that you understand your responsibilities in Canada and abide by the rules of the Canadian government.
  • UCI (Unique Client Identifier), if you have previously applied for the study permit in Canada. This number can be found in any letter issued earlier. However, no UCI is required if you are applying for the first time.

    • It is important to note that all documents should be in either English or French. If not, please get them translated in any of the two languages and proceed to the submission of the application form and fees.

Canadian Law and Justice

Candian Law and Justice

In Canada, the law applies to everyone, including:

  • Police
  • Government
  • People

Canadian laws recognize and protect basic rights and freedoms, such as liberty and equality.

Public law and private law.

Law can be divided into public and private law.

Public laws set the rules for the relationship between a person and society and for the roles of different levels of government. This includes:

  • criminal law.
  • Constitutional law.
  • administrative law

Private or civil law deals with the relationships between people. Civil laws set the rules for:

  • contracts.
  • owning property
  • rights and duties of family members
  • damage caused by others to someone or to their property

Police

The police in Canada keep people safe and enforce the law. There are different types of police, including:

  • federal
  • Provisional
  • territorial
  • Municipal

The number for your local police is in the front pages of the telephone book. If you need to call the police in an emergency, dial 911.

Rights and freedom in Canada

Learn about the Charter that protects your rights and what your rights and duties in Canada are.

The Charter protects your rights.

The Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms is part of Canadaâ€™s Constitution. It protects you from the moment you arrive in Canada.

The Charter:

  • sets out the values that Canadians live by
  • describes the kinds of personal human rights and freedoms we can expect in this country

Some of these include:
rights to:

  • life, liberty and personal security
  • a fair trial, that respects all your legal rights
  • be presumed innocent until youâ€™re proven guilty
  • talk to a lawyer as soon as possible if you are arrested
  • equal protection and benefit under the law, without discrimination

freedom of:

  • conscience and religion
  • thought, belief, opinion and expression
  • the press and other media

freedom to:

  • join groups
  • hold peaceful meetings

protection from:

  • unreasonable search or seizure
  • being detained or put in prison without a just reason

Rights come with duties.

People who live in Canada are expected to:

  • understand and obey Canadian laws.
  • help protect Canada's multicultural heritage.
  • allow other Canadians to enjoy their rights and freedoms.

If you live in Canada, you should become informed about politics and help to improve your communities and the country.

Citizens of Canada have other rights and freedoms, such as the right to vote in elections. To learn about these rights, read the Canadian Charter of Rights and Freedoms.
